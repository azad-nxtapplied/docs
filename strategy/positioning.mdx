## AI has a production problem — and in regulated industries, it has a name

Every bank, insurer, and regulated mid-market company in the DACH region is experimenting with AI. Very few are running it. The reason is rarely the technology: pilots work. What stops them at the threshold of production is a question the pilot never had to answer — **can you attest to how this AI behaves?**

Regulation has made that question concrete. The EU AI Act, sector regulation such as DORA, and supervisory practice across banking and insurance converge on the same expectations: know what your AI did and why, prove its quality before and after every change, keep humans in control of consequential decisions, and be able to show all of it — to your auditor, your supervisor, your board. Most AI initiatives cannot answer these questions today. Not because the teams are careless, but because the answers require machinery that pilots don’t build: complete records of every AI decision, evidence-based quality control, governed human oversight, and a disciplined way to improve. Without that machinery, “responsible AI” remains a policy document — and AI remains a pilot.

## What NXT is

**NXT exists to close exactly this gap. We are The AI Operations Company: we build AI into regulated operations and run it in production — governed, attestable, and getting better every cycle.**

We do not write a strategy and leave, and we do not hand you a platform and leave. We redesign the process for an AI-native world, build it into your environment, and operate it with you against measured outcomes. Build and run, not build or run — and we own the outcome, not a staffing line.

That makes us the layer of execution and control on top of the systems you already have — not another horizontal platform you buy and staff yourself. Your core systems remain the systems of record. NXT is where the routine work runs: under control, in your perimeter, with the evidence to prove it.

## What we bring

**AI-native process redesign, from validated playbooks.** For each process we serve, we bring a validated answer to “how is AI applied correctly here”: the AI-native redesign of the process itself, the AI skills that execute it, the evaluation datasets that prove their quality, the policies that constrain them, and the exception rules for everything that deviates. When we redesign your process for an AI-native world, we redesign it from a playbook we have built and are running elsewhere — not from a blank whiteboard. The design is fast because it is grounded in evidence; the result is defensible because it has been validated; and it goes live as configuration, not as a report.

**The people.** Playbooks and platforms don’t run themselves into an organization. NXT brings together an outstanding group of AI engineers, architects, and operators who have built and run AI systems in production — people who can sit across the table from your risk function and your engineers in the same meeting. They design with you, deploy in your environment, and stay engaged while the operation earns its results.

**Why we bring our own technology — the NXT Core platform.** Running AI in a regulated production environment requires machinery that pilots never build. That machinery is NXT Core, the layer on which the operation runs and stays trustworthy. We bring it because without it, build-and-run is not attestable — not because we are selling software for you to operate alone. It does three things, continuously:

- **It sees and remembers.** Every AI decision is recorded with its full context — what the model saw, which version produced the result, what it cost, what happened next. Any case can be reconstructed, any question answered with evidence. This goes beyond watching systems; it makes AI behaviour itself auditable.

- **It keeps people in command.** Your policies run as enforced gates, not guidelines. Consequential decisions route to your people with the evidence beside them; AI acts alone only within limits it has demonstrably earned — and those limits tighten automatically the moment quality slips.

- **It makes the operation better.** Every correction your team makes becomes a measured improvement: validated against reference data, released only when proven, visible in quality terms you can show your board. Oversight doesn’t end at go-live; it becomes the engine of improvement.

We build Core only where it is the source of our defensibility. Where open-source orchestration, workflow, or model tooling already does the job well, we use it. Every hour spent re-implementing what already exists is an hour not spent on the operation we are accountable for.

## Where we work

We serve regulated mid-market and PE-backed companies in the DACH region. The same underlying capability — building AI into regulated operations and running it after go-live — reaches them through three routes:

- **Regulated customer operations.** Banks and insurers running case-based work — retail banking servicing, complaint management, claims processing. NXT builds and runs the operation; your team works inside it.

- **PE portfolio operations.** Where an operating partner needs measurable AI impact inside the operationally complex businesses of a portfolio — applied where it moves the P&L, with the governance every portfolio company’s auditors and acquirers will expect.

- **Vertical AI for software vendors.** Governed AI skills embedded inside a vendor’s own product — an invoicing copilot in a medical-billing product, a completeness check inside a workflow step, a classification at the point of intake. The vendor’s software calls the skill; the result returns with an explanation, a confidence level, and a link to the full evidence.

Across all three, the work takes one of two shapes: an end-to-end regulated case operation, where work arrives, is assessed, decided, and acted upon through a purpose-built workspace; or a governed AI skill embedded in existing software. Both run on the same platform and draw on the same playbooks — an embedded skill is governed exactly like a case on a clerk’s screen.

## In your environment, with what you have

NXT deploys into your environment — your cloud or data centre, your identity provider, your security perimeter. Your data does not leave it. Your core systems remain the systems of record: we read from them with guaranteed freshness, write back only through controlled, exactly-once handoffs, and never build shadow copies of your business data.

This is also how our playbooks compound without your data ever moving. What travels across customers is method — process designs, evaluation approaches, policy patterns, and the operating model around them. Your data, your cases, and your models stay inside your perimeter. We get better at the work; we do not pool your information to do it.

If you already orchestrate processes with an established workflow platform, keep it — it answers “which step comes next,” and it does that well. NXT answers the questions it doesn’t: what did the AI see when it decided, is its quality proven, when may it act alone, and is it improving? Your existing workflows can call our skills as governed steps; your estate becomes more valuable, not redundant. Our releases travel through your change process, with evaluation evidence attached — your change advisory board gets better inputs, not a parallel universe.

And on the question that matters most in a regulated organization: **accountability stays where it belongs — with you.** No vendor can take over a bank’s responsibility for its decisions, and we don’t pretend to. What we provide is what makes that accountability carryable: an attestable governance for AI quality — the records, the evidence, the controls, and the operating model around them. We help you set it up, we operate the machinery with you, and we make sure that when your auditor asks, you answer from evidence, not from hope.

The result: a process that runs in weeks, is governed from day one, improves every month — and can be attested at any moment.
