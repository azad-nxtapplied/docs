This page gets you from zero to productive in about fifteen minutes. It explains what NXT is, who we serve, how we make money, and where we are in the build. It is the single source of truth for our high-level model. If you find something here unclear or outdated, raise it and propose a fix.

## 1. What NXT Is (in one paragraph)

**NXT is the AI Operations Company for regulated mid-market companies and PE portfolios in DACH.** We build AI systems that run real workflows for real users, and we operate those systems on retainer after they go live. We are not a consultancy. We are not a SaaS. We are a build-and-run company: we ship working AI into our clients’ operations in about ten weeks, then we run it as a recurring service while continuously improving it. We don’t digitize legacy processes — we rethink them for the age of AI, then build and run the AI-native version. Our IP accumulates in NXT Core, the process layer we use to build and operate every deployment, so that the third deployment of any pattern takes a fraction of the effort of the first.

## 2. Mission & Vision

**Mission.** Build AI that runs inside European mid-market companies and the institutions that serve them — built and operated to the standard regulated work requires, with the integrity this work deserves.

**Vision.** A Europe where AI does the work that doesn’t need a human — so the work that does can finally get the time it deserves.

## 3. Who We Serve

NXT builds and runs AI inside the operational core of DACH companies that can’t afford for it to fail — banks, insurers, PE portfolios, and the vertical software vendors that serve regulated markets. Same capability, three buyers, three GTM motions.

We pursue all three deliberately. The underlying capability is shared; the buyers and sales motions are different. Over the next 12–18 months we will rank these motions on conversion, retainer economics, reference effect, and IP capture — and concentrate where evidence points.

### Motion 1 — Regulated Customer Operations

Banks, insurers, and regulated financial services companies running high-volume customer-facing workflows: servicing, claims, Bestandsprozesse, complaints, advisory support. We deploy AI agents that handle these workflows in production with full audit trails and regulatory compliance.

- **Buyer:** Head of Operations / Head of Customer Service / CIO

- **Deal shape:** Welle 1 build phase €250–400k → run retainer €40–60k/month, scaling with additional process modules at €5–7k/month each

- **Why they buy:** Customer service costs are rising, hiring is impossible, customers expect faster service, and existing automation hasn’t worked. They need partners who can deliver inside DORA/BaFin constraints.

- **Examples in pipeline:** SWK Bank (Bestandsprozesse), Schunck (claims management for business customers), Deutsche Börse; target accounts include Sparkasse Bremen, Sparkasse Düsseldorf, N26, Apobank, Aareal Bank

### Motion 2 — PE Portfolio AI

DACH and DACH-active PE firms who need to deploy AI across portfolio companies as part of their value creation thesis.

- **Buyer:** Operating Partner, Value Creation Lead, or Portfolio CTO

- **Deal shape:** Initial scoping engagement at PE level (€150–300k) → portfolio company rollouts (€200–400k each) → run retainers

- **Why they buy:** AI is now a board-level value creation topic; portcos can’t execute alone; PE wants one trusted partner across multiple portcos rather than ten different consultancies.

- **Examples in pipeline:** Warburg Pincus / Aztec Group, Greenpeak, Moonfare, Hg, Private Assets

### Motion 3 — Vertical AI (Embedded AI for Software Vendors)

DACH software companies serving a specific industry (healthcare, hospitality, financial services, etc.) who need AI inside their product but lack the in-house capability to build and operate it.

- **Buyer:** CEO, CTO, or Head of Product

- **Deal shape:** €200–500k initial build phase → €30–50k/month run retainer

- **Why they buy:** Their customers are asking for AI; their competitors are shipping AI; they cannot hire fast enough to build it themselves; they need a partner who can build and operate.

- **Examples in pipeline:** ARZ Haan (dental software), Metro (gastro software), FNZ (financial services software), Europace

### Who We Do Not Serve

- Early-stage startups

- Companies looking for proof-of-concepts with no path to production

- Greenfield AI strategy work without a defined operational target

- One-off custom builds with no reusable architecture

- Anyone who wants to rip and replace their core systems

The discipline of three motions only works if we say no to the fourth.

## 4. The Problem We Solve

European mid-market companies, banks, insurers, and PE portfolios face the same gap: **they need AI in production, not in a slide deck, and they cannot get there alone.**

The market is full of partners who get them part of the way:

- **Strategy consultancies** produce slides but don’t ship systems

- **Implementation partners** deliver projects but don’t operate them

- **AI tools and platforms** require integration capacity these companies don’t have

- **Internal hires** take 12–18 months to assemble, and the market is empty

What’s missing is a partner who builds the system, runs it in production, takes ownership of the outcome, and improves it over time — in a regulated DACH environment, in German when needed, with the discipline these clients require. That is NXT.

## 5. NXT Core — The Process Layer

Three things make us an operations company, not a consultancy or a tools vendor:

**1. We build production systems, not prototypes.** Every engagement ships something that real users use under real load.

**2. We run what we build.** After go-live, we operate the system on a recurring retainer. Our incentives are aligned with the system working, not with selling more days.

**3. We compound IP.** Every deployment feeds NXT Core, our internal platform. This third point is the strategic core of the company and deserves real depth.

### We build AI-native processes, not AI-enabled legacy ones

The single biggest mistake in enterprise AI today is bolting AI onto processes that were designed for humans, paper, and pre-internet software. The result is marginal efficiency gains and frustrated stakeholders.

We do the opposite. Every engagement starts with the question: if this process were designed today, knowing what AI can do, what would it look like? Often the answer eliminates entire steps, collapses approval chains, reverses the direction of work, or turns a process that used to be batch into something that runs continuously.

This is harder than digitizing what exists. It requires real understanding of the business, not just the workflow, and the willingness to say no to scope that preserves legacy decisions for political reasons. It’s also where the real value lives — and where competitors who think AI is just “smarter automation” lose.

### What NXT Core is — and is not

**NXT Core is not a horizontal AI platform.** We do not compete with foundation model providers, orchestration frameworks, vector databases, or observability vendors. That layer is crowded with well-funded players, and we use what they build.

**NXT Core is the process layer** — the components that sit between the platform layer below and the operational process inside our customers. Our IP is in how AI gets assembled into a working, regulated, run-able operational process. Everything horizontal we can reuse from open source, we do.

NXT Core is owned by NXT Core GmbH (the IP-holding entity). Client deployments happen through NXT Applied GmbH, which licenses Core.

### The six components

Every deployment uses these six. Every deployment also extends them. That’s how Core compounds.

**1. Workspace — the human surface of NXT Core.** The interface where business users review, approve, correct, and override AI-driven work. Not a chat window, not a copilot pane. A workspace designed for the operational patterns that recur across our three motions — case queues, exception review, approval flows, audit-ready correction capture. AI shows up inside the workflow, not as a separate destination. Every correction feeds the learning loop.

**2. Process Playbooks — codified operational patterns.** Not “experience” but tangible, reusable assets: process templates per case type (Bestandsprozess, claims FNOL, AP exception, dental case routing, etc.), exception taxonomies, decision trees, escalation matrices, SOPs. These are the patterns we’ve seen work in production — codified so deployment four runs from the package rather than the discovery call.

**3. AI Components — the intelligence inside the process.** The modular AI units that do the cognitive work — classification, extraction, recommendation, drafting, decisioning — built with a clear distinction between deterministic steps (rules, logic, calculations) and non-deterministic steps (LLM calls, judgment, ambiguity). Designed for production: self-monitoring, self-healing where possible, with runtime evals that catch quality drift before it reaches a customer. Built to reduce — not increase — the number of humans needed to run the process.

**4. Integration Layer — connection to the legacy estate.** We know how to integrate AI into the messy reality of mid-market and regulated environments: legacy ERPs (DATEV, Scopevisio, Lexoffice, SAP), core banking systems, CRMs, document stores, on-prem databases, ticketing systems, communication platforms. Every connector becomes reusable IP. Integration failures surface as cases, not outages.

**5. Audit Trail & Observability — regulation built in.** Every AI decision recorded: model, prompt version, input, output, confidence, downstream user action, outcome. Replay engine. Decision logs aligned to BaFin, DORA, MaRisk, GDPR, and EU AI Act requirements. Most competitors bolt compliance on; we build the audit substrate as the foundation, because it’s also the foundation of the learning loop.

**6. Operational Economics — running AI affordably and predictably.** LLMs are expensive when used badly. NXT Core steers which model handles which step based on task complexity, latency budget, and cost — deterministic logic where possible, small models where they suffice, large models only where they earn it. We track token cost, latency, failure cost, and human-touch cost per case. This is what makes the run retainer economics work over time.

### Our position on the platform layer underneath

NXT does not build a horizontal platform. We build the process layer that sits on top of one. Underneath the six NXT Core components runs the platform stack: foundation model gateways, orchestration engines, observability tools, data infrastructure, vector stores. That layer is crowded with strong players. We don’t compete there.

**We run one canonical reference stack** — assembled from open-source and commercial best-of-breed components — that every deployment uses. This is what makes the third deployment of any pattern faster than the first. One stack to know deeply, one to operate at quality, one to hire and train against.

**Where customer environments substitute** — model gateway (e.g., the customer’s own Azure OpenAI tenancy), observability tooling (e.g., a customer mandates Datadog), identity and SSO — we have a defined adapter layer. These substitutions are explicit and bounded. Where they do not — orchestration, eval pipeline, secrets management, cost tracking, audit log — Core remains canonical, because operating quality requires consistency.

We use open source where we can, contribute upstream where it matters, and do not fork. Specific component choices are owned by the Tech Lead and documented in 02 Product/Engineering.

### Our position on existing workflow / BPM engines

Many customers — especially banks, insurers, and regulated enterprises — already run a BPM engine (Camunda is the dominant one in DACH financial services; others include Pega, SAP, and in-house orchestrators). How NXT relates to these engines is strategic, not technical. Our position is coexistence with defined boundaries:

- **NXT Core owns orchestration for new AI-native workflows** — the part where the learning loop, the cost steering, and the audit substrate live.

- **Existing BPM engines continue to own existing flows** — we do not rip and replace.

- **The two exchange events** at clean boundaries. Where existing flows need AI augmentation, NXT exposes specific AI components as service tasks the BPM can call.

What we do not do is embed NXT Core as a service task inside someone else’s BPM as our primary deployment mode. The orchestration layer is where the learning loop lives, and the learning loop is the strategic asset. If we lose orchestration, we lose the platform thesis. For customers without significant existing BPM investment (most vertical software vendors, many PE portfolio companies), NXT Core owns orchestration end-to-end — the cleaner deployment shape.

This position is non-negotiable in scope-setting. We will walk away from scope that requires subordinating the new AI-native work to a third-party orchestration engine. We will not walk away from a customer; we will redefine what we deliver for them.

### How this is different from what others build

- **Process-shaped, not chat-shaped.** Most AI tools are chat-first or copilot-first. NXT is workflow-first. AI runs inside the process; the process is the unit of work, not the conversation.

- **Regulated by design, not by retrofit.** Audit trail, EU data residency, explainability, and human-in-the-loop are foundational, not optional features.

- **Built for run, not for demo.** Observability, cost management, self-healing, and runtime evals exist because we operate what we build. Competitors optimize for impressive demos; we optimize for systems that run reliably for years.

- **Process layer, not platform layer.** We use open-source and commercial infrastructure underneath. We don’t compete with Anthropic, OpenAI, LangChain, or n8n. We sit on top of them.

- **Compounding through deployment, not through fundraising.** Every engagement makes Core sharper. We don’t need to raise capital to build features; our customers fund them.

## 6. Commercial Model

**Build phase.** A ten-week structured engagement. Fixed scope, fixed price (€200–500k depending on complexity and motion). Output is a working production system, evaluated against criteria agreed at kickoff.

**Run phase.** After go-live, we operate the system on a recurring retainer (€30–60k/month depending on scope and complexity). The retainer covers operating the system, monitoring quality, handling exceptions, evolving the system as the business changes, and reporting on outcomes.

Why this works commercially:

- **Predictable revenue** — run retainers compound into recurring ARR

- **Aligned incentives** — we make money when the system works, not when we extend the engagement

- **Compounding IP** — every retainer funds further investment in NXT Core

- **Real moats** — once a system is in production and we operate it, switching costs are high

## 8. How We Are Different

Five deliberate trade-offs that define us:

**1. Build and run, not one or the other.** Most competitors either build (consultancies, agencies) or run (SaaS, managed services). We do both. This is operationally harder but commercially much stronger.

**2. Senior team, small headcount.** We hire only senior people who have shipped before. No junior layer to manage. Quality and velocity over scale.

**3. DACH-native, regulated-fluent.** We operate in German when needed, understand BaFin / DORA / GDPR, and have credibility with German-language clients that English-first competitors lack.

**4. IP capture by discipline.** Every engagement is deliberately architected so that 60%+ of the underlying system is reusable. Most services firms claim this; few execute on it.

**5. Founder-led sales for now.** Our PE channel and high-trust mid-market sales motion require founder presence. We are not optimizing for “scalable sales” prematurely.

## 11. Our Principles (What Is Fixed)

**1. Outcome ownership.** We are responsible for what we build, not just for delivering it.

**2. Senior people only.** No junior layer in the next 18 months. We hire from “seen them ship.”

**3. AI-native, not AI-bolted-on.** We rethink processes for the age of AI before we build them, and we do not preserve legacy steps just because they exist — for our customers as well as our internal processes.

**4. Process layer, not platform layer.** We sit above the platform stack and don’t compete with it. We compound IP through deployment, not through fundraising. We own orchestration for AI-native workflows; we coexist with customer BPM at defined event boundaries.

**5. Regulated by design.** We assume GDPR, DORA, BaFin, and the EU AI Act from day one — not as an afterthought.

**6. Founder-led sales until further notice.** Sales is a strategic function, not a delegated one.

**7. Speed and quality, in that order.** We ship fast and we ship working. If we have to choose, we delay rather than degrade.

**8. No discount selling.** Our pricing reflects what we deliver. We walk away from price-driven deals.

If a decision conflicts with these, challenge it.

## 14. FAQ (Short)

**Are we a consultancy?** No. We build production AI systems and operate them on retainer. We do not bill by the hour, sell decks, or staff projects. Saying “consultancy” anywhere in client conversations is wrong.

**Are we a SaaS company?** No, not yet. We are a build-and-run company that is deliberately compounding IP in NXT Core. In 24–36 months, parts of Core may become productized SKUs. Today, we sell engagements, not products.

**Are we a platform company?** We are a process layer company. The platform layer below us (models, orchestration frameworks, observability tools) is crowded with strong players. We don’t compete there; we sit above it and assemble it into working operational processes inside customer environments.

**Three motions sounds broad. Are we focused?** Yes — because the underlying work is one thing: building AI into regulated operations and running it after launch. The three motions are different buyers for the same capability. We say no to any opportunity that doesn’t fit one of the three, and we will narrow further as evidence accumulates.

**Why no VC?** Because we don’t need it and it would distort the company shape. With the bootstrap setup and the run-retainer model, NXT can self-fund to €5M+ ARR. VC would force premature scaling and a different exit profile.

**What are we building toward?** A solid mid-market business — durable, profitable, and self-sustaining, with recurring run retainers, real IP in Core, a senior team, and no dependency on external capital. We optimize for a business that works on its own terms, not for a flashy exit story. If exit optionality emerges over time because we built something real, that’s a consequence, not the plan.

**What is the single most important thing in the next 90 days?** Close SWK Welle 1 cleanly and convert it into a run retainer by September. Every other 2026 outcome depends on this one.
