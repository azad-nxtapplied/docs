NXT Core is an AI-native platform that runs inside the customer's environment. It is organized into four **bands** — from the customer-facing playbooks at the top down to the AI kernel — all resting on a shared **Foundation** (Platform Core). The bands hold the AI-specific machinery; the Foundation is the generic plumbing that every band uses. Each band builds on the bands below it, and everything builds on the Foundation.

**Hover any box** for a quick summary; the **kernel and foundation boxes** (marked ↗) open their own page. The sections below explain how the pieces fit together, deploy, and improve.

## From positioning to architecture

NXT is the AI Operations Company: it makes AI work in regulated industries — governed, attestable, and improving every cycle. Each promise is an engineering requirement, and the architecture is the minimal structure that satisfies all of them at once:

- **Attestable** → nothing happens without producing its own record.
- **Governed** → the customer's policies are executable gates, and autonomy is an earned, reversible state.
- **Getting better every cycle** → every change is measured against reference data before release, and corrections flow back as validated improvements.
- **Proven playbooks** → process knowledge is a versioned, testable, deployable artifact, not project code.
- **One governance for two solution kinds** → an embedded API call is governed exactly like a case on a clerk's screen — same ledger, same gates, same evidence.
- **Your environment, your systems** → the platform deploys self-contained into the customer's perimeter and treats their systems as the system of record.

The four bands and the Foundation beneath them are what those six requirements demand — no more, no less.

## How the layers relate

The platform is a stack of dependency layers whose dependencies only ever point **downward** — each layer builds on what is beneath it, never sideways into another vertical and never up. From the bottom: the generic **Foundation** carries the plumbing every layer uses; the **Kernel** adds the AI guarantees and machinery, identical in every deployment; the **AI Suites** assemble the kernel into the two ways of working behind one Platform API; the **Playbooks** band is where each industry lives, as versioned, release-gated configuration; and **Deployment** is the only layer that knows the individual customer.

The boundary between the bottom two bands is the sharp one: remove a kernel element and a *guarantee* breaks; remove a suite element and a *way of working* breaks. Industry language first appears in the Playbooks band, and the customer first appears in Deployment — so everything below stays standard, certifiable, and identical across deployments.

## Skills and components

Two terms recur throughout. A **Skill** is a capability given to the AI — a taught, versioned unit of work (classify a complaint, check billing completeness, draft a response). A **Component** is a solution built for a customer that runs on the platform — composed of skills, policies, and configuration. Components are built from skills; playbooks define and validate both.

## How it deploys and operates

**One instance per customer.** The whole stack runs as a single self-contained instance inside the customer's environment, on their infrastructure, federated to their identity, storing to their object store. Instances are reproducible from configuration — one platform, many instances, never forks.

**The operating split: the customer operates the system; NXT operates the AI.** The customer runs the infrastructure and first-level operations. NXT operates the quality cycle — evaluation, drift response, prompt and playbook evolution, autonomy management — from outside the perimeter, on an allow-listed stream of **quality signals only** (scores, metrics, error signatures — never business content).

**Three integration modes** govern how it coexists with what the customer already runs: NXT runs the process end to end (Way 1); the customer's own software calls governed components as steps (Way 2); or a split, where NXT runs the AI-native segment and hands deterministic tails back through the Integration Fabric.

## Tenancy

Multi-tenancy lives **inside** an instance: customers are isolated at the instance level, and **workspaces** isolate teams — or, in a partner deployment, the partner's downstream customers — within it. Every request carries a `workspace_id`, resolved by IAM and scoped by the Foundation. In a partner deployment, a machine credential is bound to exactly one workspace and cannot address another, and the partner's end users travel as recorded `on_behalf_of` claims rather than platform accounts.

## Build vs borrow

NXT owns everything that carries the guarantees and the playbook IP — the governance semantics of the kernel elements, the playbook format, and the service and connector contracts. Everything beneath is proven open-source infrastructure, self-hosted in the customer's perimeter: PostgreSQL, Redis, Kubernetes, a Keycloak-class identity broker, an OSS LLM proxy and model-serving runtime behind the Model Gateway, pgvector behind AI Memory, the customer's own object store. The line is deliberate: **NXT builds the governance and adopts the plumbing.**

## Key decisions

- **A modular monolith**, not microservices — one instance per customer makes per-service operational overhead a cost without a benefit.
- **One PostgreSQL per instance**, organized by concern, with the dependency rules mirrored in the data layer.
- **One versioned Platform API** as the single external surface — NXT's own surfaces consume the same public API, with no private backdoor.
- **Serve-from-state** for interactive responses — results are computed behind the scenes and read back fast, rather than running a live pipeline per request.
- **Case orchestration is the product**, so no external workflow engine is adopted; coexistence is solved by the embedded (Way 2) path instead.
- **SaaS is deliberately not built** — the door is held open (workspace-scoped everything, instance-from-configuration, billing-grade metering), but nothing is built behind it until a product decision exists.

## Vision

NXT Core is the AI Operations Platform: the governed execution layer on which AI works in regulated industries — running proven playbooks on top of existing systems, in the customer's own environment, never replacing what already works. Every decision is recorded with what the AI saw. Every change is validated before release. Every autonomy level is earned on evidence. The customer's team stays in command, the customer's accountability is answered from evidence, and every deployment makes the next one faster.
