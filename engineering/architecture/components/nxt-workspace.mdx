NXT Workspace is the **operator surface** — for humans *in* the loop. It is where people do the work alongside the AI: picking up a case, reviewing the AI's proposal beside its evidence, and approving, correcting, or overriding it.

## What it shows

- **Queues / inbox** — the cases waiting for a person, prioritized and routed.
- **Case detail** — the AI's proposal with **its reasoning and the evidence beside it**, so the operator can judge it, not just trust it.
- **Gate-review screens** — the checkpoints where a human decides: review-and-send, confirm-resolve.

## Every action counts

From a case, the operator can **approve, reject, correct, or override** — and every one of those is recorded, and **every correction becomes a learning signal**. The point of the Workspace is not just to close the case, but to capture the human judgment that makes the AI better next time.

## In the running example

For the duplicate-charge complaint: the operator opens the parked case, sees the AI's drafted refund reply with the billing statement and a confidence beside it, softens the tone, and approves. The case resumes and the reply is sent; the operator's edit flows back as a correction.

## Just another consumer

The Workspace is a **first-party app over the Platform API** — it has no private access. It calls the same public API a partner's software would; if it can do its job on the public contract, so can anyone.

## Dependencies

A consumer of the [Platform API](/engineering/architecture/components/platform-api). Through it, operator actions reach the [Agent Case Framework](/engineering/architecture/components/agent-case-framework) (to resume a parked case), and corrections become signals for the [Learning Engine](/engineering/architecture/components/learning-engine). Every action is recorded in the [AI Auditor](/engineering/architecture/components/ai-auditor).
