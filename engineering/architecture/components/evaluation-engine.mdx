The Evaluation Engine measures **how good the AI is, on evidence**. It scores behaviour against known-correct reference cases, gates every change before it ships, and watches live traffic for drift. It produces the evidence that the word "earned" refers to when autonomy is earned.

## What it owns

The Engine owns the **golden datasets** (curated reference cases with known-correct outcomes), the **release gates** every change must pass, and **drift detection** on live traffic.

## It gates every change

No prompt, policy, knowledge version, or playbook ships without passing its declared thresholds against the golden set. A proposed change is run against the reference cases and **blocked if quality regresses** — which is what makes "no silent changes" a property of the platform rather than a promise.

## It watches live quality

Beyond release, the Engine scores live work and detects drift, and human corrections — an edited draft, an overridden decision — become quality signals. So quality is measured continuously, not just at the moment something ships.

## It measures, it doesn't change

The Evaluation Engine produces the evidence; it does not change behaviour itself. The [Learning Engine](/engineering/architecture/components/learning-engine) proposes the improvements, and the [Autonomy Controller](/engineering/architecture/components/autonomy-controller) turns the evidence into how much autonomy is allowed.

## Dependencies

Reads outputs and outcomes from the [AI Auditor](/engineering/architecture/components/ai-auditor) stream; gates proposed changes both in the build pipeline and at per-environment promotion; and feeds scores to the [Learning Engine](/engineering/architecture/components/learning-engine) and the [Autonomy Controller](/engineering/architecture/components/autonomy-controller). It runs largely as background jobs on an adopted evaluation substrate, with the governance on top kept as NXT's.
