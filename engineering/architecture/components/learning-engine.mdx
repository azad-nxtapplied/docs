The Learning Engine is **how the platform gets better, every cycle** — governedly. It captures corrections and outcomes, turns them into proposed improvements, and computes the earned trust that autonomy is built on. It is the "run" NXT operates as a service across every deployment.

## What it owns

The Engine owns the capture of **corrections, overrides, and outcomes** as structured signals; their curation into **datasets and golden-set candidates**; the **proposals** for better prompts, policies, and playbooks; and the computation of **earned trust** per capability.

## It proposes, it never ships silently

Every improvement the Learning Engine proposes is released **only through the Evaluation Engine's gates**. It can suggest a better prompt, but it cannot put that prompt into production on its own — the change must first prove itself against the golden set. Improvement and governance stay coupled.

## It turns track record into trust

As good outcomes accumulate, the Engine computes a capability's **earned trust** — the evidence the [Autonomy Controller](/engineering/architecture/components/autonomy-controller) reads when deciding whether a capability has earned more autonomy. This is the loop that makes autonomy something earned rather than configured.

## The run, operated from outside

This is the engine NXT operates across all deployments as the ongoing quality cycle — curating golden sets, evolving prompts and playbooks — working from quality signals, never from business content.

## Dependencies

Consumes correction signals from the [AI Auditor](/engineering/architecture/components/ai-auditor) stream and from human actions in the Workspace (Band 2); routes every proposed change through the [Evaluation Engine](/engineering/architecture/components/evaluation-engine); and feeds earned trust to the [Autonomy Controller](/engineering/architecture/components/autonomy-controller). It runs as background and batch jobs.
