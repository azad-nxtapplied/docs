NXT Admin is the **administrator surface** — where a customer's administrators configure and govern their deployment. It is the user interface onto Band-4 configuration: the values and choices that make one instance *theirs*.

## What it manages

- **Users and roles** — who may do what (the RBAC roles).
- **Policy values** — the thresholds and limits the Policy Gate enforces (a refund ceiling, an approval threshold).
- **Autonomy ceilings** — the maximum tier a capability may be promoted to.
- **Connector credentials** — the keys for the customer's systems.
- **Branding** — the instance's look.

## Configuration, governed like everything else

Admin writes **Band-4 configuration**, exportable as **one reviewable bundle** — the basis of instance reproducibility and safe change. Every change an admin makes is **recorded like any other action** and **gated by RBAC**, so configuring the platform is itself governed and attestable.

## In the running example

Before any complaint runs, an admin sets *"refunds ≤ €25 are auto-approvable"* as a policy value and caps the billing-complaint capability's autonomy ceiling. Those settings are what the Policy Gate and Autonomy Controller later enforce on every case — and the change itself is in the record.

## Where it sits with the Cockpit

Admin sets the **standing configuration** (values, ceilings, credentials); the [Cockpit](/engineering/architecture/components/nxt-cockpit) makes the **evidence-based decisions** (promote a tier when quality earns it). One configures; the other governs on evidence.

## Dependencies

A first-party app over the [Platform API](/engineering/architecture/components/platform-api). It writes configuration to [Configuration & Secrets](/engineering/architecture/components/configuration-secrets) and the policy/autonomy settings, all scoped and authorized through [IAM](/engineering/architecture/components/iam)'s RBAC, and every change is recorded in the [AI Auditor](/engineering/architecture/components/ai-auditor).
