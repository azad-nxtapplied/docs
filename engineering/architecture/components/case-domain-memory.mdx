Case Domain Memory is the **case engine's state** — the case-scoped operational record that answers *"where does this case stand right now?"* It is the data the [Agent Case Framework](/engineering/architecture/components/agent-case-framework) reads and updates as a case moves through its lifecycle.

## What it owns

For each case, it holds the operational record:
- the **classification** and extracted **entities**,
- the **work items, exceptions, and approvals**,
- the **communication thread**,
- and the **parked state** — where the case is waiting, and for what.

## A case-shaped view over AI Memory

This is the distinction to hold:
- **AI Memory** (kernel) is the substrate — knowledge, the context assembled for a task, and evidence.
- **Case Domain Memory** is the *operational state of one case* on top of it — not *what the AI knows*, but *where the case stands*.

The evidence behind a decision lives in AI Memory; the case's status, thread, and parked point live here. (It's the Way-1 sibling of Component Memory: one is a case's record, the other a component's track record.)

## Part of the case engine

Case Domain Memory is not a standalone service — it is the **state half of the case engine**. The Agent Case Framework owns the behavior (lifecycle, park/resume) and is the single writer of case status; Case Domain Memory is the record that behavior reads and updates.

## Dependencies

Stored through the [Data Adapter](/engineering/architecture/components/data-adapter); views evidence and context from the kernel's [AI Memory](/engineering/architecture/components/ai-memory); and every change to it is a recorded execution in the [AI Auditor](/engineering/architecture/components/ai-auditor). It is written by the [Agent Case Framework](/engineering/architecture/components/agent-case-framework) as it advances a case.
